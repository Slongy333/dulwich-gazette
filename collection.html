About This Edition

The Dulwich Gazette was produced in honour of Professor Antony Long's sixtieth birthday on the 3rd of March, 2026. It is modelled upon the tradition of Edwardian polar journalism — the broadsheet as expedition record, the newspaper as monument to human endeavour.

Professor Long is a Distinguished Old Alleyn's Scholar of Dulwich College, a glaciologist of international renown, Vice-Chancellor of Durham University, veteran of expeditions to Greenland and Spitsbergen, and a man who once carried a colleague eight miles across a crevassed ice-field with a polar bear at two hundred yards. The Gazette considers this the minimum standard for a sixtieth birthday tribute.

The photographic record is drawn from the archive of Frank Hurley, official photographer of the Imperial Trans-Antarctic Expedition, 1914–17, by kind permission of the Royal Geographical Society.
