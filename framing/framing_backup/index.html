The Horizontal Triptych

Three frames, side by side — the complete broadsheet experience.

Charcoal Wall · Dark Oak Frames · Ivory Mount ★ Recommended

Why This Works

This is the classic broadsheet display. Three frames of equal size, hung in a horizontal row with the masthead page at centre, create a wall installation that reads like a genuine Edwardian newspaper spread out on a reading table — except it never yellows, never tears, and never gets used to line a birdcage.

The dark charcoal or forest green wall throws the aged cream paper into sharp relief. The eye travels naturally left to right, reading the narrative across all three sheets. Guests will stop. They will lean in. They will ask questions. That is the point.

The Gazette's Recommendation

Specifications

  • Three frames, each 297 × 750mm (A3 width, custom height)
  • Frame: dark antique oak or antique black, 20–25mm moulding
  • Mount: ivory or natural cream, 40–50mm border
  • Wall colour: charcoal, forest green, or deep navy
  • Spacing between frames: 30–50mm
  • Total wall width required: approx. 1.1–1.2m
  • Hang height: centre of frames at eye level (approx. 145–155cm)
  • Print finish: matte or satin on 170gsm uncoated stock
B

Dramatic · The Staircase Edition

The Vertical Column

Three frames stacked in a single column — built for tall walls and staircases.

Forest Green Wall · Antique Black Frames · Staircase Setting

Why This Works

The staircase is one of the most underused display spaces in a house — and one of the most dramatic. Three frames hung vertically, with the masthead at the top, create a column of narrative that unfolds as you ascend. By the time you reach the top, you have read the whole story.

This option works particularly well in a Victorian or Edwardian property — which, given the renovation underway near Thirsk, may be rather apt. The forest green wall is period-perfect. The antique black frames are crisp against it. The wall sconce provides exactly the right quality of warm, directional light.

Excellent for Tall Walls & Staircases

Specifications

  • Three frames, each 297 × 750mm (A3 width, custom height)
  • Frame: antique black or dark walnut, 20mm moulding
  • Mount: ivory or natural cream, 40mm border
  • Wall colour: forest green, deep burgundy, or charcoal
  • Spacing between frames: 30–40mm
  • Total wall height required: approx. 2.6–2.8m
  • Top frame: hang so its centre is at approx. 200cm
  • Print finish: matte on 170gsm uncoated stock
C

Bold · The Statement Piece

The Single Hero

One large frame, one wall, one statement. The masthead page as a centrepiece.

Navy Wall · Dark Oak Frame · Wide Ivory Mount · Library Setting

Why This Works

Sometimes one thing, done at scale, is more powerful than three things done at normal size. A single large frame — A2 or even A1 — of the masthead page above a sofa, fireplace, or desk makes an immediate and unambiguous statement. The Dulwich Gazette as a centrepiece, not a collection.

This is the right choice if wall space is limited, if budget is a consideration, or if Antony simply has one wall that deserves a single, commanding piece. The wide ivory mount gives the print room to breathe. The navy wall turns the whole thing into something that looks like it belongs in a Pall Mall club.

Best for a Single Statement Wall

Specifications

  • Single frame, 420 × 900mm (A2 width) or 594 × 1200mm (A1)
  • Frame: dark antique oak, substantial 30–35mm moulding
  • Mount: wide ivory, 60–80mm border — give it space
  • Wall colour: deep navy, charcoal, or dark forest green
  • Hang height: centre of frame at eye level (approx. 145–155cm)
  • Print finish: matte or satin on 200gsm uncoated stock
  • Note: request print at 100% scale — do not auto-fit
  • PDF file: Poster 1 (the masthead page) is the one to use

Designer Briefing Notes

  • Files are image-based PDFs (rasterised at 150dpi). Print at 100% — do not scale or auto-fit.
  • Request matte or satin finish. Glossy will destroy the aged paper aesthetic.
  • Preferred stock: 170gsm uncoated for the newsprint feel; 200gsm for the single hero option.
  • The background colour is a warm cream (#F3E8D0) — ensure the printer's white point is calibrated accordingly.
  • For custom frame sizes, a framer can cut bespoke mounts to fit these non-standard dimensions.
  • Recommended framers for bespoke work: Snap Galleries (London), The Frame Workshop (York — close to Thirsk), or any FATG-accredited framer.
